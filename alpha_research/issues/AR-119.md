## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** When broad, liquid mega-cap equities show capitulation-like abnormal volume and downside range expansion but close-location/breadth begins improving, the forced-selling liquidity imbalance may resolve over the next 3-10 sessions. Trading diversified broad/defensive ETFs or cash should capture recovery with lower single-name idiosyncratic risk than direct mega-cap baskets.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Constituent-level OHLCV breadth can reveal market-wide liquidity exhaustion before ETF-level allocation fully normalizes. The intended driver is broad liquidity/microstructure recovery after selloff, not country ETF stale-price catch-up, sector earnings pressure, or generic ETF momentum.
- **Expected failure mode:** The signal becomes an AR-043/AR-046 clone, over-gates into sparse zero exposure, churns like AR-103, or duplicates rejected constituent-confirmed sector pressure from AR-115.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Signal breadth from 50-100 liquid US mega-cap equities across sectors selected ex ante using Alpaca/qfa daily OHLCV coverage, sufficient history from 2018 where possible, dollar-volume/liquidity, and sector diversity. Tradable ETF pool: SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLY, XLI, XLP, XLU, XLB, XLRE, TLT, IEF, SHY, GLD, USMV, QUAL, and similar Alpaca-covered liquid ETFs.
- **Product-selection filters:** Alpaca daily coverage/history, liquidity/volume sanity, exposure diversity, and economic mapping before performance review.
- **Selected universe:** To be selected by the research subagent before performance review; signal universe should retain broad sector diversity and tradable universe should be ETF/cash only.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Uses single-name breadth as a signal but diversified ETF/cash instruments for risk expression. Limitations include survivorship risk from mega-cap selection, daily-bar timing granularity, and potential redundancy with existing liquidity-reversal alphas.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only; no CSV; no `--data-csv`; no daemon; no orders.
- **Signal features:** Mega-cap abnormal share/dollar volume z-scores; downside range expansion / realized range z-scores; close-location recovery after downside range days; market-wide and sector-level breadth of exhaustion and improvement; 3/5/10 day decay/cooldown after breadth thrust; ETF allocation preference among broad equity, defensive sectors, duration/gold, or cash/no exposure.
- **Model form:** Rule-based breadth signal that trades ETF/cash sleeves.
- **Parameters / search space:** Volume/range z-score windows; close-location thresholds; breadth-thrust thresholds; 3/5/10 day holding decay; sector/broad ETF mapping; cooldown; top ETF sleeve count.
- **Position sizing:** Long-only ETF/cash allocation with exposure scaled by breadth strength and defensive fallback; avoid forced full exposure on weak/no signal.
- **Risk constraints:** Turnover/cooldown, cash/no-exposure fallback, sleeve caps, cost sensitivity, activation guardrails.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** 2018-present where Alpaca coverage permits; 20-30 random/stress windows if runtime allows, minimum 10.
- **Metrics:** Median/mean/p25/worst Sharpe, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover, activation rate, holding-period turnover, positive-window rate, sector/sleeve attribution.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 5, 10, and 20 bps one-way turnover-cost proxy; 10 bps primary decision threshold.
- **Ablations:** ETF-only AR-043-style stress liquidity/range/volume signal; mega-cap breadth signal trading broad ETFs only; sector-level constituent breadth trading mapped sector ETFs; no close-location recovery; no abnormal-volume filter; no range-expansion filter; no breadth-thrust filter; no defensive/cash fallback; direct mega-cap basket comparator; simple SPY/QQQ/IWM/TLT/GLD defensive benchmark.
