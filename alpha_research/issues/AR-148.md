## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamped U.S. Treasury auction result metrics — high-yield/award-rate surprise, bid-to-cover, and bidder-takedown mix — may reveal demand/supply shocks that create next-session to 5-day drift or fade in duration, credit, and rate-sensitive ETFs.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Auction outcomes reveal real-time marginal demand for duration. Weak demand or high award-rate surprises may trigger delayed duration weakness or risk-off effects; strong demand may support duration and rate-sensitive sleeves.
- Expected failure mode: This collapses to rejected auction-calendar/date-only Treasury effects; true when-issued tail is unavailable; conservative next-session lag removes edge; simple duration TSMOM/reversal or rate beta controls dominate; one rate regime drives results.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Liquid U.S. ETFs with direct or indirect rate exposure: SHY, IEI, IEF, TLT, GOVT, EDV/ZROZ if coverage/liquidity pass; TIPS ETFs such as TIP/SCHP; credit/MBS ETFs such as LQD/HYG/MBB/AGG/BND; rate-sensitive equity sectors such as XLF/KRE, XLU, XLRE, plus SPY/QQQ controls.
- Product-selection filters: qfa/Alpaca real daily bar coverage, liquidity, and economic exposure only; no return-based selection.
- Selected universe: To be selected after source feasibility and coverage check; nominal notes/bonds primary, TIPS diagnostic unless event count is sufficient.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Duration/rate-sensitive ETFs should be affected by Treasury demand shocks; credit/equity sleeves are controls/diagnostics, not rescue levers.
- Data source: U.S. Treasury FiscalData auction results API or equivalent official public source, plus qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV. Use conservative next-regular-session entry unless a machine-readable results-publication timestamp is documented.
- Signal features: Security type/term, high_yield/award-rate history z-score by type/term, bid_to_cover_ratio z-score, indirect/direct/primary-dealer accepted share z-scores, inflation-indexed flag, auction date, closing time. True when-issued tail only if timestamp-safe WI source is available; otherwise do not claim WI tail.
- Model form: Feasibility-first event-study allocator / strict fast-falsification.
- Parameters / search space: Fixed primary leg next session through 5 trading days; nominal note/bond sleeves separated; diagnostics for TIPS; 10 bps primary cost with 5/20 bps sensitivity.
- Position sizing: Equal-weight or volatility-scaled duration/rate-sensitive sleeves; cash outside events; cap term/sleeve concentration.
- Risk constraints: No same-day close trading unless result timestamp is proven; exclude/diagnose overlapping FOMC/CPI/payroll days; no daemon/trades.
