## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** AR-045's cost-aware abnormal volume + extreme close-location reversal edge may generalize beyond the original mega-cap sleeve when universe expansion is controlled by ex-ante liquidity, event activation, turnover, and overlap gates rather than return selection.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Closing/benchmark-flow dislocations can create short-lived price pressure that normalizes over the next 1-5 sessions. The edge should be strongest where abnormal daily volume and close-location extremity identify forced flow while liquidity remains sufficient to absorb costs.
- **Expected failure mode:** Broadening recreates noisy/high-turnover rejected variants such as AR-068 or sector ETF failures such as AR-095/AR-098; activation becomes too sparse; or the return stream is redundant with AR-028/AR-045/AR-056.
- **Falsifier:** Median random-window Sharpe <= 0 after 10 bps costs, p25 Sharpe < 0, turnover above preset cap, activation outside floor/ceiling in most windows, or max relevant correlation > 0.60 to retained liquidity-reversal/watchlist alphas.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Alpaca/qfa real-data liquid US sector/industry ETFs plus 50-100 liquid large-cap constituents selected ex ante by price, dollar-volume proxy, history length, and data availability.
- **Product-selection filters:** Liquidity/spread proxy, sufficient event count, direct close-volume-dislocation exposure, stable history coverage, no return-ranked selection, and no near-duplicate concentration.
- **Selected universe:** To be selected from the candidate pool before performance evaluation; sector/industry ETFs are auxiliary sleeves unless they independently pass activation and cost gates.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Uses daily OHLCV abnormal full-day volume and close-location as a proxy; qfa/Alpaca daily bars do not provide true closing-auction imbalance data.
- **Data source:** qfa with Alpaca real market data only; no CSV-backed research.
- **Signal features:** Volume z-score, close-location extremity, short-horizon reversal score, realized-volatility normalization, liquidity/spread proxy, event activation filter, overlap/correlation screen.
- **Model form:** Rule-based qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` with explicit event gating and turnover throttle.
- **Parameters / search space:** Volume z-score thresholds, close-location thresholds, 1-5 day holding/rebalance cadence, volatility window, event activation bounds, single-name/sleeve caps, 5/10/20 bps costs.
- **Position sizing:** Gross exposure <= 1.0; cap per symbol and per sleeve; avoid simultaneous high-correlation ETF/constituent exposures doubling the same bet.
- **Risk constraints:** No daemon, no orders; no raw data/SQLite artifacts retained; compact evaluation summaries only.
