# AR-133 — Exact same-index ETF pair/triplet residual convergence

## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Exact or near-identical benchmark ETF substitutes can temporarily diverge in lagged log price ratios; sufficiently large residual z-score extremes should converge over 3-5 trading days after transaction costs.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Same-index ETF substitutes have stronger economic linkage than generic ETF mean-reversion or sponsor/exposure sleeves. The test asks whether daily close-to-close residual deviations are large and persistent enough to survive realistic costs with timestamp-safe next-bar execution.
- Expected failure mode: Residual amplitudes are too small versus costs, turnover dominates, the raw ratio/simple ETF reversal placebo beats, or the universe collapses to too few clusters.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: US-listed liquid ETFs with exact or near-identical benchmark exposure, checked before performance review. Primary clusters: S&P 500 SPY/IVV/VOO/SPLG; Nasdaq-100 QQQ/QQQM if coverage sufficient; Russell 2000 IWM/VTWO; total US market VTI/ITOT/SCHB; aggregate bonds AGG/BND.
- Product-selection filters: qfa/Alpaca daily OHLCV coverage, sufficient overlapping history, liquidity/median dollar volume, documented benchmark identity or near-identity, cluster size >=2.
- Selected universe: To be selected from the above clusters before performance review; exclude non-identical near-exposure pairs from the primary test.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Prefer exact substitute pairs/triplets only; if coverage leaves too few clusters, mark limited/rejected rather than broaden into generic ETF mean reversion.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no daemon, no orders. No NAV/premium/fund-flow claims.
- Signal features: Lagged log price ratio residual z-scores within exact substitute clusters, residual amplitude diagnostics, normalization/exit rule based on past closes only.
- Model form: Rule-based pair/triplet residual convergence allocator.
- Parameters / search space: lookback 60/126/252 trading days; z threshold 1.0/1.5/2.0; holding period 3/5 days; amplitude floor; max cluster gross exposure.
- Position sizing: Dollar-neutral within cluster, long cheap/short rich residual outliers, normalized weights with per-symbol and per-cluster caps.
- Risk constraints: Next-bar/next-close timestamp safety; turnover and cost tracking; reject if average residual entry amplitude is not comfortably above modeled costs.

## Evaluation Plan
- Random-period protocol: Multiple random windows from 2018-present where overlapping coverage permits, with smoke test first.
- Metrics: median/p25/worst random-window Sharpe, mean Sharpe, positive-window rate, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover, hit rate, activation rate, residual amplitude diagnostics.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: 10 bps primary; 5 bps diagnostic; 20 bps sensitivity.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: Compare to watchlist alphas if primary gates pass; if decisively rejected, mark deferred_due_rejection.
- Required ablations/placebos: raw pair z-score, simple ETF reversal/mean reversion, AR-002/011 ETF mean reversion, AR-005/019 residual pair logic, AR-121 liquidity pressure, AR-132 sponsor/exposure residual dispersion.
- Acceptance threshold: 10 bps median random-window Sharpe >0, p25 >0, positive-window rate >=55%, residual amplitude clears costs, not beaten by raw/simple placebo, acceptable turnover/drawdown, no 20 bps cost collapse.
