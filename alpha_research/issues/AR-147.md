## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamp-safe weekly Federal Reserve H.4.1 balance-sheet component changes may create delayed liquidity/risk-appetite impulses across broad liquid equity, credit, duration, gold, and cash-proxy ETFs when traded only after the public Thursday post-close release.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Changes in reserve balances, reverse repo usage, Treasury General Account, and total Fed assets alter market liquidity plumbing, collateral scarcity, and cross-asset risk budgets. The effect, if present, should be observable only after the public release and should beat generic risk-on/off, TSMOM, and calendar controls.
- Expected failure mode: Signal is stale, crowded, confounded by FOMC/month-end/Treasury calendars, or duplicates generic risk-off, duration/carry, ETF TSMOM, AR-042 macro-flow, or AR-063 drawdown-state allocators.
- Falsifier: Timestamp/vintage feasibility fails; same-day unreleased H.4.1 values are required; post-cost random-window p25 is negative; shifted or inverted labels perform similarly; controls explain returns; or max relevant correlation exceeds 0.60.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Broad liquid ETFs with economic exposure to liquidity/risk appetite: SPY, QQQ, IWM, HYG, LQD, TLT, IEF, SHY, GLD, UUP or another USD proxy if available, and a cash/SHY fallback.
- Product-selection filters: Liquidity, qfa/Alpaca daily coverage, clean economic exposure, and fixed ex-ante selection. No return-ranked symbol selection.
- Selected universe: To be fixed before scoring from the candidate pool by coverage and economic exposure; document any unavailable USD/cash proxy.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Broad ETF sleeves give liquid capacity-friendly exposures to equity beta, credit risk, duration, gold, and cash/dollar defensive states. Weekly macro releases limit sample size and may be regime-dependent.
- Data source: FRB H.4.1 release archive plus FRED/ALFRED point-in-time or conservative release-lag series for WALCL, WRESBAL, WTREGEN, RRPONTSYD or release-aligned equivalents; Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV for ETFs. No CSV-backed market data, no qfa daemon, no orders.
- Signal features: Weekly released changes/z-scores in reserve balances, RRP, TGA, total Fed assets, and a predeclared net-liquidity proxy if timestamp-safe. Positive/negative liquidity impulse buckets; no optimized continuous return fitting before ablations.
- Model form: Rule-based weekly ETF allocator with conservative next-session entry after Thursday 4:30 p.m. ET release or holiday-adjusted release.
- Parameters / search space: Primary 1-week hold/rebalance; 2-week diagnostic only; 10 bps one-way primary cost; 5/20 bps sensitivity; limited predeclared z-score windows.
- Position sizing: Gross exposure <=1.0, single ETF <=35%, cash/SHY fallback.
- Risk constraints: Never use same-day Wednesday H.4.1 observation before publication; align daily RRP to weekly publication availability if used; exclude or control FOMC weeks, month-end/turn-of-month, and Treasury calendar overlaps.
