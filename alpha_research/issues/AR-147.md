## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamped public weekly financial-conditions/stress indicators such as Chicago Fed NFCI/ANFCI or St. Louis Fed financial stress series may identify exogenous credit/liquidity-condition shocks that produce delayed ETF risk-off drift or relief reversal over 5–10 trading days.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Official weekly stress/financial-conditions updates summarize cross-market credit and liquidity pressure. If investors rebalance slowly or use these releases as confirmation, broad ETFs may exhibit delayed de-risking after tightening shocks or relief after easing shocks.
- Expected failure mode: Signal is just a generic risk-off/defensive-carry clone; FRED observation dates are mistaken for release timestamps; revisions/vintages leak future information; ETF TSMOM or price-only stress controls dominate; performance concentrates in one crisis.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Broad liquid U.S.-listed ETFs across equity beta, sectors, factors, credit, duration/TIPS, gold/commodities, USD/FX proxies, and cash-like sleeves with qfa/Alpaca real daily bars.
- Product-selection filters: liquidity/coverage/economic exposure only; no return-based selection; select sleeves before performance scoring.
- Selected universe: To be selected after confirming source timestamp/release-lag feasibility and qfa/Alpaca coverage.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Broad cross-asset ETF pool is required because financial-conditions shocks can affect equities, credit, duration, USD, commodities, and defensive/carry sleeves differently. Risk of redundancy with prior ETF risk-off/defensive allocators must be measured explicitly.
- Data source: Official/FRED/Chicago Fed/St. Louis Fed weekly financial-conditions or stress series with documented publication/release lag or conservative lag convention; qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV. Do not trade on FRED observation date alone unless publication timing is proven.
- Signal features: Predeclared primary official series; weekly change/shock z-score; fixed direction before testing; diagnostics for alternative series only if primary is not rescued by them.
- Model form: Rule-based external-signal ETF allocator / strict fast-falsification.
- Parameters / search space: One primary source and lag convention; fixed 5–10 trading day horizon; 10 bps primary cost; 5/20 bps sensitivity; limited threshold diagnostics only.
- Position sizing: Equal-weight or volatility-scaled ETF sleeves; cash when no qualifying signal; sleeve caps to avoid one asset class dominating.
- Risk constraints: Source timestamp discipline; no future revisions/vintages; no same-day release trading unless release time is verified; no daemon/trades.
