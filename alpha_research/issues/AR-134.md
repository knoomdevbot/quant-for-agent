## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamped SEC Form 4 filings that disclose clustered open-market insider purchases by officers/directors may produce underreaction drift over 5-20 trading days after the public filing acceptance time.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Multiple insiders buying in the open market is a cleaner informed-signal than sales or deterministic filing calendars. Smaller/liquid-but-underfollowed issuers may incorporate the signal slowly.
- Expected failure mode: The anomaly is already crowded, mostly priced between transaction date and filing acceptance, dominated by small-cap/value/distress exposure, or disappears after costs and placebo controls.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Broad U.S. common-stock issuers with SEC Form 4 XML filings since 2018 and qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV coverage. Use SEC CIK/ticker mapping plus Alpaca asset/data availability; exclude ETFs/funds/ADRs/preferreds/warrants/OTC when identifiable.
- Product-selection filters: SEC Form 4 coverage; non-derivative open-market purchase transaction code `P` with acquisition flag `A`; officer/director role; minimum price and median dollar-volume filters; unambiguous ticker mapping; sufficient event count; qfa/Alpaca data availability.
- Selected universe: To be selected by the evaluator after event-source feasibility and liquidity/data filters, targeting liquid small/mid/large U.S. common stocks with enough events. Do not select by realized returns.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Universe is matched to the informed-insider-purchase mechanism. Current CIK/ticker mapping and active Alpaca tradability may introduce survivorship/ticker-change bias; ambiguous mappings must be dropped or explicitly labeled.
- Data source: SEC EDGAR public filings/API for event timestamps and Form 4 XML transaction fields; qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV for market data. No CSV-backed market data and no `--data-csv`.
- Signal features: Filing acceptance timestamp; issuer/ticker; distinct officer/director buyer count; transaction code; purchase-value proxies if available; pre-filing return/volatility/liquidity diagnostics; entry date computed conservatively from public acceptance time.
- Model form: Event-study long-only rule-based signal with optional market/sector residual diagnostics.
- Parameters / search space: Primary cluster threshold >=2 distinct officers/directors with open-market purchases accepted within 5 trading days; diagnostics for >=3 insiders/10 trading days, CEO/CFO-only, director-only, and single-purchase baseline; hold periods 5/10/20 trading days; costs 10 bps primary with 5/20 bps sensitivity.
- Position sizing: Equal-weight active event sleeve with caps per issuer/date; cash when no valid events. Optional market-neutral diagnostic against SPY/IWM if implementation supports it.
- Risk constraints: Maximum concurrent issuer cap, minimum liquidity/price, no same-day lookahead, no use of transaction date as knowledge time, event/year/sector concentration diagnostics.

## Evaluation Plan
- Random-period protocol: Feasibility gate first. If timestamp-safe event construction passes, run random event/time windows across the available SEC/qfa overlap, including leave-one-year-out and event-month randomization diagnostics.
- Metrics: Sharpe, Sortino if available, annualized return, vol, max drawdown, turnover, hit rate, positive-window rate, event count, activation rate, concentration by year/sector/name, capacity proxy by dollar volume.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: 10 bps one-way primary; 5 bps and 20 bps sensitivity.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: Compare daily/event return stream against accepted/watchlist alphas where available, especially ETF TSMOM, pairs residual mean reversion, turn-of-month, and any single-name/event watchlist streams. If rejected decisively before library comparison, mark orthogonality deferred due rejection.
- Acceptance threshold: Positive 10 bps median random-window Sharpe; p25 not materially negative; positive-window rate >=55%; beats single-purchase, generic any-Form-4 filing, generic post-filing drift, shifted filing date, random matched non-event, and small-cap/value/momentum/reversal baselines; no one year/sector/name dominates; max relevant correlation <=0.60; 20 bps stress does not fully collapse.
