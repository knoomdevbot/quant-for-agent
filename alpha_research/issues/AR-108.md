## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Scheduled Treasury auction/refunding supply weeks can create temporary duration and curve ETF pressure; when long-duration ETFs underperform short-duration/cash-like ETFs into predeclared supply windows and then stabilize on lagged volume/close-location confirmation, a 3-10 day allocator may capture supply-concession reversal.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Treasury issuance and refunding windows can concentrate rate-duration supply and dealer balance-sheet pressure. A strictly predeclared event calendar plus stabilization confirmation may separate supply concession from generic duration carry, FOMC/CPI announcement reversal, or risk-off rotation.
- **Expected failure mode:** The signal repeats rejected duration/carry or announcement-event variants, is redundant with AR-037/AR-049/AR-061/AR-063/AR-105, works only in one rate regime/year, or is actually turn-of-month/month-end/risk-off exposure in disguise.
- **Falsifier:** 10 bps random-window median Sharpe <= 0, materially negative p25 Sharpe, max relevant correlation > 0.60 (preferably > 0.50), performance concentrated in one year/refunding cycle, or ablations show the return is generic ToM/month-end/risk-off rather than auction/refunding supply pressure.

- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real market data only; no CSV-backed research.
- **Signal features:** Predeclared monthly/quarterly Treasury auction/refunding windows, lagged duration-versus-cash/short-duration relative return, volume/close-location stabilization, realized volatility/risk-off skip filters, and explicit post-window hold days.
- **Model form:** Rule-based qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` event-calendar allocator.
- **Parameters / search space:** Auction/refunding window offsets, 3/5/10 day hold, relative-return thresholds, stabilization confirmation thresholds, volatility/risk-off filters, ETF caps, gross exposure cap, and 5/10/20 bps costs.
- **Position sizing:** Gross exposure <= 1.0, no leverage, duration and credit sleeve caps, risk-off/defensive allocations only as filters or hedges when explicitly specified.
