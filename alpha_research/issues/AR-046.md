## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Instead of fading single-name extreme close-location events, cross-sectional liquidity exhaustion breadth across mega-caps may predict a short-horizon recovery basket after broad forced selling/buying pressure.
- **Economic intuition:** Simultaneous abnormal range/volume events across multiple liquid mega-caps can indicate temporary liquidity demand imbalance that reverts at the basket level, a different mechanism from AR-028's per-name close-location fade.
- **Expected failure mode:** Breadth proxy is redundant with market beta/volatility, costs erase short-horizon recovery, or extreme regimes trend rather than mean-revert.
- **Falsifier:** Non-positive median random-period Sharpe after costs, unacceptable drawdown, or high correlation with AR-028/market mean-reversion streams.

## Implementation Plan
- **Universe:** liquid mega-cap equities using Alpaca/qfa daily OHLCV.
- **Data source:** Alpaca/qfa real market data only; no CSV-backed research.
- **Signal features:** Cross-sectional count/intensity of abnormal volume, abnormal range, and downside/upside close-location exhaustion; basket-level recovery allocation rather than individual event fading.
- **Model form:** qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` returning symbol weights.
- **Risk constraints:** no live trading; no daemon; no orders.

## Evaluation Plan
- One primary qfa/Alpaca backtest plus 6-12 random windows; avoid long grid searches.
- Document costs/slippage and the lack of true auction imbalance data.
- Compare against AR-028 and available accepted/watchlist return streams.
