## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** ETF flow pressure may be better captured around scheduled macro/liquidity dates and cross-asset breadth shifts than around calendar month boundaries.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Rebalancing and risk-budget changes are not only month-end phenomena; FOMC/CPI/payroll/liquidity-risk periods can drive repeatable positioning in equities, duration, and gold.
- **Expected failure mode:** Event calendars unavailable through qfa/Alpaca, proxy event logic is too noisy, costs erode sparse signals, or returns are redundant with existing macro ETF rotations.
- **Falsifier:** Median random-period Sharpe <= 0 after costs, severe drawdowns, or high redundancy versus stronger watchlist alphas.

## Implementation Plan
- **Universe:** liquid ETFs such as SPY, QQQ, IWM, TLT, GLD and optionally sector ETFs if justified.
- **Data source:** Alpaca/qfa real OHLCV only; no CSV-backed research.
- **Signal features:** Cross-asset breadth, realized volatility, equity-duration-gold relative pressure, and scheduled-date proxies that can be computed without external CSV fixtures.
- **Model form:** qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` returning symbol weights.
- **Parameters / search space:** small prespecified windows only; avoid long grid searches.
- **Position sizing:** Normalize gross exposure to 1.0 with concentration caps and optional cash state.
- **Risk constraints:** No live trading; no qfa daemon; include 5 bps turnover-cost haircut if qfa lacks native costs.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** Required, 6-12 random windows on Alpaca/qfa real data.
- **Metrics:** Sharpe, annualized return, annualized volatility, max drawdown, win rate, periods, cost/turnover proxy.

- **Acceptance threshold:** Positive median random-window Sharpe after costs and lower correlation to AR-021 than another turn-of-month refinement.
