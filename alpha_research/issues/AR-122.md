## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** On predeclared broad market macro-shock days identified only from timestamp-safe daily OHLCV, sectors/ETFs showing relative stabilization versus cyclicals and credit over the next 3-10 sessions forecast a short-horizon low-beta recovery/rotation effect distinct from generic defensive carry, ETF TSMOM, and AR-118 pullback recovery.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** During equity/credit/duration stress, defensive sectors that stop deteriorating relative to cyclicals may reveal de-risking exhaustion and subsequent low-beta recovery demand. The signal must be event-gated, not continuous defensive allocation.
- **Expected failure mode:** The effect is another redundant defensive/carry/stress allocator, only works in one crisis cluster, fails after 10-20 bps costs, or is explained by sector relative strength/mean reversion, ETF TSMOM, AR-118-like pullback recovery, or AR-063/105-style stress allocation.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Sectors/industries (XLK, XLY, XLP, XLV, XLU, XLF, XLI, XLE, XLB, XLRE, XLC plus liquid industry ETFs if coverage is clean), broad/style ETFs (SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA, USMV, SPLV, QUAL, MTUM), credit/duration (HYG, LQD, SHY, IEF, TLT, TIP), and hedges/commodities (GLD, DBC/XLE as available).
- **Product-selection filters:** Real daily OHLCV coverage, liquidity, sufficient history, exposure-family diversity, and pre-performance universe fixation. No return-based symbol selection.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed by the research subagent before performance review.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Broad enough to distinguish relative stabilization from generic defensive/carry allocation and sector-only mean reversion; limited to daily OHLCV proxies and cannot use external macro calendars unless timestamp-safe and explicitly available.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** Macro-shock day from SPY bottom-tail 1-3 day drawdown, HYG credit weakness, TLT hedge failure/duration stress, and realized-volatility jump. Stabilization features include defensive-sector drawdown rank vs cyclicals, close-location/range compression after shock, 3-10 day relative vol compression, defensive-vs-cyclical recovery breadth, and HYG/LQD or HYG/IEF confirmation.
- **Model form:** Rule-based event-study allocator active only around shock windows, with cash/SHY fallback and explicit activation tracking.
- **Parameters / search space:** Shock thresholds; stabilization lookback 1/3/5 days; holding periods 3/5/10 days; top-N stabilized ETFs; cooldown and crisis-cluster handling.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-weight or volatility-scaled low-beta/recovery sleeve, concentration caps, and cash fallback.
- **Risk constraints:** Primary 10 bps one-way turnover cost with 5 and 20 bps stress; reject one-crisis dominance and high redundancy.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** Random windows across available real daily history plus shock-event stratification and leave-one-crisis/cluster-out checks where feasible.
- **Metrics:** Median/mean/p25/worst Sharpe, annualized return/vol, max drawdown, turnover, activation rate, independent shock clusters, hit rate, positive-window rate, and 5/10/20 bps cost sensitivity.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 10 bps primary; 5 and 20 bps stress.
- **Ablations:** No shock gate, shock gate only/no stabilization, generic defensive sector carry/rank, sector relative strength, sector mean reversion after drawdown, AR-118-like pullback proxy, AR-063/105-style stress allocator proxy, ETF TSMOM, carry/defensive rotation, month-end/turn-of-month controls, crisis-exclusion checks.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** Compare against AR-118, AR-105, AR-063, AR-051, AR-107, AR-109, AR-098, AR-037, and AR-049 where compact return/proxy data are available.
- **Acceptance threshold:** At 10 bps, median random-window Sharpe > 0, p25 preferably >= 0 and not materially negative, positive-window rate > 55-60%, max relevant correlation <= 0.60 hard / <= 0.50 preferred, beats defensive carry/rank, ETF TSMOM, AR-118-like pullback, and AR-063/105-style stress proxies, and is not one-crisis dominated.
