## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Abnormal close-to-close tracking residuals in leveraged/inverse ETF pairs versus their unlevered underlying ETFs can identify short-lived hedging/rebalance stress that predicts next-day reversal or continuation in the underlying ETF.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Leveraged and inverse ETFs mechanically rebalance exposures and can experience liquidity/hedging stress when realized moves, volume, and range expand. Pair-level residuals versus stated leverage may contain more specific stress information than generic volume/range pressure in the underlying ETF.
- **Expected failure mode:** The residual is only compounding/fee/dividend noise, stale pricing, realized-volatility beta, or a clone of rejected OHLCV liquidity/stress alphas (AR-043/088/119/121). It may fail after costs, be dominated by crisis years, or become redundant with simple one-day reversal/momentum.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Broad ex-ante set of liquid unlevered ETFs with related leveraged/inverse products: broad index, growth/tech, small-cap, Treasury, financials, energy, technology, semiconductors, biotech, real estate, retail/homebuilders, and other sectors where bull/bear leveraged or inverse products have sufficient Alpaca daily OHLCV coverage.
- **Product-selection filters:** Predeclare underlying/leveraged/inverse mapping before performance review; require real Alpaca/qfa daily OHLCV coverage, minimum overlapping history, sufficient liquidity, valid stated leverage multiple/orientation, and no performance-based inclusion. Exclude mappings with too little overlap or inactive products.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed by the research subagent before performance review. Primary trades only unlevered underlying ETFs; leveraged/inverse ETFs are signal inputs only.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Pair residuals target a more specific ETF mechanism than generic OHLCV stress, but daily bars cannot observe actual borrow, creations/redemptions, NAV premium/discount, holdings, or rebalance trades. Product inception/closure and active-date differences create survivorship/regime limitations.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** Close-D tracking residual between leveraged/inverse ETF return and stated multiple times underlying ETF return; optional same-day volume/range stress gate or scale; realized volatility and market-regime controls. All features must be shifted so close-D information creates D+1 positions only.
- **Model form:** Rule-based event/stress allocator over unlevered underlying ETFs. Report separate reversal and continuation sleeves; no ex-post blend unless each sleeve independently clears gates.
- **Parameters / search space:** Residual z-score lookbacks 20/60/126 days; residual threshold 1.0/1.5/2.0 sigma; holding period 1/3 days; optional volume/range confirmation; gross exposure caps; 10 bps one-way primary costs with 20 bps stress.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-risk or capped equal-weight positions in underlying ETFs only; cap single ETF exposure; cash when no qualifying residual event.
- **Risk constraints:** Reject if selected mapping count is too small for broad inference, if performance comes from one crisis/product, if turnover/cost sensitivity destroys results, or if max correlation to related watchlist alphas exceeds 0.60.
