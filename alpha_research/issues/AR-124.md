## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** A first or early 252-trading-day high in a sector/industry ETF after a cooldown may reflect investable underreaction only when related ETF peer/cluster breadth is expanding; isolated single-ETF highs without peer confirmation may be exhaustion or should be avoided/faded.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Sector and industry leadership can diffuse across economically related ETFs as investors rotate into a newly strengthening theme. A first high after a long cooldown with peer confirmation may capture delayed allocation flows, while isolated highs may be idiosyncratic/crowded and less persistent.
- **Expected failure mode:** The signal is merely raw 126/252-day TSMOM or a continuous breadth allocator clone; event count is too sparse; one cluster/year dominates; cluster definitions are ex-post; isolated-high diagnostics are used to rescue a weak continuation sleeve; turnover/costs erase edge; correlations to AR-015/AR-077/AR-083/AR-104/defensive-carry alphas breach gates.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Broad ex-ante ETF pool across sector SPDRs, liquid industry ETFs, broad indexes, factors/styles, defensive/cash/bond proxies, commodities, gold/miners, real assets, and international proxies available through qfa/Alpaca daily OHLCV.
- **Product-selection filters:** Real Alpaca/qfa daily OHLCV coverage, sufficient 252-day history, liquidity proxy/median dollar-volume, cluster economic coherence, minimum cluster size, no performance-selected membership, no CSV fixtures.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed by the research subagent before performance review. Predeclare clusters such as sector SPDRs, semiconductors/technology, biotech/healthcare, banks/financials, metals/miners/materials, homebuilders/real estate, transports/industrials, broad/factor/style, duration/credit/defensive, and commodities where coverage/liquidity pass.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Broad product discovery is required so the event is not tested only on a default ETF basket. Cluster definitions must be timestamp-safe and economic rather than performance-mined. ETF history/survivorship and changing ETF availability remain limitations.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** First/early 252-trading-day high after at least a 63-trading-day cooldown; cluster peer confirmation threshold of roughly 40-50% of peers above predeclared 126-day/252-day high or trend thresholds; trailing 126/252-day return controls for residualization; isolated-high flag for separate diagnostic fade/avoidance sleeve.
- **Model form:** Rule-based sparse event study / event allocator. Primary sleeve is peer-confirmed new-high continuation. Isolated-high fade/avoidance is diagnostic and must not be blended into the primary result.
- **Parameters / search space:** Primary defaults: 252-day high, 63-day cooldown, 40-50% peer confirmation, 10-trading-day hold, max per-cluster exposure cap. Minor neighboring diagnostics may be reported but cannot rescue the primary if it fails.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-weight or volatility-scaled positions across active confirmed events with cluster and gross exposure caps; report turnover and clustered simultaneous entries.
- **Risk constraints:** 10 bps primary one-way turnover cost with 5 and 20 bps sensitivities; reject if minimum event count is too low, if one year/cluster dominates, if residualized performance vanishes, or if max relevant correlation exceeds 0.60.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** Random windows plus sparse event-year and cluster-leaveout diagnostics across the full eligible qfa/Alpaca daily history. Evaluate all usable first-high events after fixed universe/cluster selection. Include leave-one-year-out and leave-one-cluster-out robustness.
- **Metrics:** Median Sharpe or event-return distribution where Sharpe is low-power, p25/worst event return or Sharpe, positive-window/event rate, annualized return/vol if meaningful, max drawdown during holds, turnover, activation/event count, cluster/year concentration, cost sensitivity.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 10 bps primary; 5 and 20 bps stress.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** Compare to AR-003/AR-015 ETF TSMOM, AR-036 rejected regime brake, AR-104 broad ETF TSMOM cluster, AR-077 ETF trend breadth, AR-083 sector breadth trend, AR-057 breakout, AR-037/AR-049/AR-051 defensive/carry/rotation, and low-vol/defensive baselines where compact streams/proxies are available.
- **Acceptance threshold:** Positive median after 10 bps, non-materially-negative p25, acceptable drawdown/turnover, sufficient event count, no year/cluster dominance, beats raw 252-day high breakout and 126/252-day TSMOM/breadth/carry baselines, and max relevant correlation <= 0.60. Hard reject if performance disappears after conditioning/residualizing on trailing 126/252-day returns.
