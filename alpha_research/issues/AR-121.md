## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Across a broad, preselected universe of liquid ETFs, family-relative daily OHLCV dislocations identify temporary liquidity pressure that mean-reverts over 1-5 trading days.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** ETF close-location pressure, abnormal dollar-volume/turnover, gap/close residuals, realized range expansion, and return-volume dislocations may reflect temporary liquidity demand. Creation/redemption pressure is only unobserved intuition; it is not a measured signal.
- **Expected failure mode:** Daily OHLCV proxies are too weak/noisy, turnover/costs erase any reversal, performance is explained by simple ETF mean reversion, ETF TSMOM, or defensive/carry allocation, or results clone prior liquidity-reversal alphas.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Broad liquid ETF pool selected before performance from sector, industry, bond, credit, commodity, international, factor, style, and broad index ETFs with Alpaca/qfa daily coverage.
- **Product-selection filters:** Real daily OHLCV coverage, median dollar-volume/liquidity sanity, exposure-family diversity, sufficient history, no return-based symbol selection.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed by the research subagent before performance review using coverage/liquidity/exposure filters.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Broad family-relative design is required to test whether ETF-level liquidity pressure is distinct from single-name close-volume reversal and from narrow default ETF baskets. It cannot observe true ETF flows, NAV, or premium/discount.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** Abnormal dollar-volume/turnover shock, close-location pressure, gap/close residual, realized range expansion, return-volume dislocation, family-relative normalization, 1/3/5 day reversal horizons.
- **Model form:** Rule-based ETF allocator; compare long-only/cash fallback and market-neutral-lite if feasible.
- **Parameters / search space:** Lookbacks 20/60/120 days for normalization; dislocation thresholds; holding periods 1/3/5 days; top/bottom N by family; max turnover/cooldown constraints.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-weight or volatility-scaled among selected dislocation candidates with concentration caps and cash fallback.
- **Risk constraints:** Primary 10 bps one-way turnover cost; 5 and 20 bps stress; reject if turnover resembles AR-103-style cost fragility.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** Random windows across available real daily history, with regime coverage where available. Use selected universe fixed before performance.
- **Metrics:** Median/mean/p25/worst Sharpe, annualized return/vol, max drawdown, turnover, activation, hit rate, positive-window rate, 5/10/20 bps cost sensitivity.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 10 bps primary; 5 and 20 bps stress.
- **Ablations:** ETF-only close-volume reversal, family mean reversion, ETF TSMOM, defensive/carry allocator, equal-weight/risk-off proxy, no abnormal volume, no close-location, no gap/close residual, no range expansion, raw vs family-relative signals, 1/3/5 day horizons.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** Compare against AR-028, AR-045, AR-056, AR-103, AR-119, AR-106, AR-015, AR-037, AR-049, and AR-051 where compact return/proxy data are available.
- **Acceptance threshold:** At 10 bps, median random-window Sharpe > 0, p25 not materially negative, positive-window rate >= 55%, 20 bps stress not fully collapsed, full model beats simple ETF reversal/family mean-reversion/TSMOM/defensive baselines, and max relevant correlation <= 0.60 hard / <= 0.50 preferred.
