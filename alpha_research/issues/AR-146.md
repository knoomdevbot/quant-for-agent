## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Actual timestamped SEC 8-K items related to restructurings, exits/disposals, material charges, and impairments — primarily Item 2.05 and Item 2.06 — may identify discrete negative-fundamental reassessment events that produce post-filing underreaction drift or overreaction reversal in liquid U.S. common stocks.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: These filings reveal management-confirmed restructuring/impairment decisions rather than generic filing-calendar attention. Investors may either underreact to persistent fundamental deterioration or overreact to one-time charges.
- Expected failure mode: Event-source construction fails; signal reduces to generic negative-news/post-event reversal; results concentrate in crisis years/sectors/distress names; costs and sparse events overwhelm; item text is too noisy or dominated by earnings/M&A overlap.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: All liquid U.S. common-stock issuers with EDGAR timestamped 8-K / 8-K/A filings, item metadata/text, unambiguous issuer CIK/ticker mapping, and qfa/Alpaca real daily bars.
- Product-selection filters: common stocks only; exclude ETFs/funds/ADRs/preferreds/warrants/OTC where identifiable; minimum price/liquidity/coverage; no return-based selection.
- Selected universe: To be selected after event-source feasibility, before performance scoring. Block before performance if fewer than 200 timestamp-safe liquid common-stock events survive.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Uses actual timestamped filing content rather than deterministic filing calendars; issuer/ticker mapping, delistings, amendments, and event concentration must be reported explicitly.
- Data source: SEC EDGAR filing metadata, acceptance timestamps, 8-K item codes, and 8-K item text only; Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV. Do not use deterministic filing calendars, current compiled companyfacts as historical PIT data, or fabricated event proxies.
- Signal features: Item 2.05 / Item 2.06 flags, restructuring/impairment/exit/disposal/material-charge text confirmation, acceptance-time-to-next-bar mapping, amendment/duplicate clustering, earnings-overlap exclusion diagnostics.
- Model form: Feasibility-first event study / allocator; performance only after source gate passes. Drift and reversal variants must be separated rather than ex-post blended.
- Parameters / search space: fixed primary hold window chosen before performance; diagnostics for 1d/5d/10d/20d if feasible; 10 bps primary cost with 5/20 bps sensitivity.
- Position sizing: equal-weight or equal-risk active event baskets with issuer/year/sector caps; cash when no events.
- Risk constraints: SEC acceptance datetime is knowledge time; if after market close/weekend/holiday or session eligibility is ambiguous, enter no earlier than the next eligible daily bar. No lookahead from later amended filings or current fundamentals.

## Evaluation Plan
- Random-period protocol: Stage 1 feasibility report: candidate filings, item-code parse rate, text fetch/parse rate, CIK/ticker mapping success, qfa/Alpaca coverage, liquidity-qualified events, duplicate/amendment drops, item/sector/year distribution, acceptance-time coverage. Stage 2 performance only if Stage 1 passes, using random event-containing windows.
- Metrics: full and random event-window Sharpe after 10 bps, median/p25/worst random-window Sharpe, positive-window rate, hit rate, max drawdown, turnover, event count, activation, year/sector/name concentration.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: primary 10 bps one-way; 5/20 bps sensitivity if feasible.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: hard controls against AR-130 filing-calendar attention, AR-134 Form 4 blocked event-source precedent, AR-139 companyfacts PIT blocker, generic momentum/reversal, shifted filing labels, matched non-item 8-Ks, generic post-8-K drift, sector/beta residual controls, and Item 2.02 earnings overlap.
- Acceptance threshold: source gate passes with >=200 events; positive costed median Sharpe; p25 not materially negative; primary beats matched non-item 8-Ks, shifted labels, and generic momentum/reversal; no single year/sector/event cluster dominance; 20 bps does not fully collapse.
