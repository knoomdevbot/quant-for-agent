## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamped SEC Schedule 13D/13G initial blockholder disclosures may create post-filing drift or overreaction/reversal in liquid U.S. common stocks when traded only after a conservative EDGAR accepted-time lag.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: A new 5%+ activist, strategic, or passive blockholder disclosure can concentrate investor attention and reveal informed positioning or governance intent; delayed market digestion may create short-horizon drift, while crowded/high-attention names may overreact.
- Expected failure mode: The signal reduces to generic filing-calendar attention, pre-filing run-up reversal, momentum/reversal, market/sector beta, size/value/distress, meme-stock clusters, or parser/ticker-mapping artifacts.
- Falsifier: If a timestamp-safe event table cannot be built with sufficient liquid initial 13D/13G events after parsing/mapping/deduplication gates, block/reject without performance testing. If performance later fails after costs versus matched non-events, shifted labels, market/sector/momentum/reversal controls, and AR-130 filing-calendar controls, reject.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: All U.S. exchange-listed common-stock subject issuers appearing in SEC Schedule 13D/13G filings over the qfa/Alpaca overlap, with EDGAR accepted timestamps and reconstructible subject-issuer CIK/ticker/security mapping.
- Product-selection filters: SC 13D or SC 13G primary forms for initial-event candidates; exclude SC 13D/A and SC 13G/A from the primary sleeve; parse subject issuer CIK/ticker/security class, filer/group identity, ownership percentage >=5%, accession number, acceptance timestamp, and primary document URL; require qfa/Alpaca bar coverage and liquid common-stock-like instruments; exclude ETFs/funds/ADRs/preferreds/warrants/OTC/ambiguous class shares where identifiable.
- Selected universe: To be fixed before scoring. Feasibility gate requires at least 200 liquid initial 13D/13G events across non-dominated issuers, filer groups, years, and months before any performance backtest.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: EDGAR accepted timestamps are point-in-time anchors, but current SEC ticker mappings are not historical security masters; ticker changes, delistings, multiple share classes, group filings, and duplicate amendments must be recorded as limitations.
- Data source: SEC company tickers/submissions/companyfacts or filing documents only for event metadata; qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only if the feasibility gate passes. No CSV-backed market data, no qfa daemon, no orders.
- Signal features: form type, accepted timestamp, initial-vs-amendment flag, filer/group identity, parsed ownership percent, activist/passive 13D-vs-13G sleeve, pre-filing run-up diagnostics, next-session lag.
- Model form: Feasibility-first event-table scout; later rule-based event drift/reversal model only after source gates pass.
- Parameters / search space: Primary initial 13D/13G event sleeve; 1d/5d/10d/20d hold diagnostics; 10 bps one-way primary cost plus 5/20 bps sensitivity; conservative next-session entry.
- Position sizing: Equal-weight active event basket with issuer, filer/group, sector, and event-date caps; cash when no qualifying events.
- Risk constraints: No same-day lookahead; no current-only ticker mapping without caveat; no return-based selection; cap clustered meme/distressed/mega-cap event days.

## Evaluation Plan
- Random-period protocol: Feasibility gate first. If passed, evaluate random event-containing windows and leave-one-year-out diagnostics over the timestamp-safe source/qfa overlap.
- Metrics: median/p25/worst random-window Sharpe, positive-window rate, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover, hit rate, activation/event count, issuer/filer/year/month concentration, cost sensitivity.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: primary 10 bps one-way; 5/20 bps sensitivity.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: Compare against AR-130 13F filing-calendar rejection, AR-134 Form 4 blocked event-source precedent, AR-138 FTD, AR-139 XBRL, AR-145 8-K feasibility block, generic event drift, momentum/reversal, sector/beta residuals, matched non-events, shifted labels, pre-filing run-up, and buy-hold/market beta.
- Acceptance threshold: Feasibility gate passes; positive post-cost median random-window Sharpe; p25 not materially negative; positive-window rate >=55%; 20 bps stress does not collapse; primary sleeve beats matched non-events, shifted labels, filing-calendar, momentum/reversal, market/sector controls; no single issuer/filer/year/month dominates; max relevant correlation <=0.60.

## Preflight Rationale
Independent preflight returned `queue_after_revision_feasibility_scout_only` with score 21/30 and medium-low confidence. A lightweight SEC metadata probe found 10,433 company ticker mappings and, across 18 current issuers, 531 recent SC 13D/13G filings including 112 initial-form candidates with acceptance timestamps. The idea is materially different from rejected AR-130 13F calendar attention because it uses actual filing contents/events, but local SEC-event priors are hostile and parser/PIT security-master risk is high. The queued version is therefore feasibility-first with hard source, parsing, mapping, ownership, breadth, concentration, and qfa/Alpaca coverage gates before any performance evaluation.
