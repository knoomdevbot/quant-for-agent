## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** AR-021's exchange-session-aware turn-of-month edge may improve if exposure is reduced during high-volatility or risk-off regimes that drove negative random windows.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Month-boundary flow effects can be overwhelmed by broad deleveraging; a simple realized-volatility / trend gate may preserve the flow premium while avoiding stressed regimes.
- **Expected failure mode:** Added gate overfits recent market regimes, reduces sparse positive exposure too much, or remains redundant with AR-021/AR-006.
- **Falsifier:** Median random-period Sharpe <= 0 after documented costs, worse drawdown than AR-021, or no improvement in lower-tail windows.

## Implementation Plan
- **Universe:** SPY, QQQ, IWM, TLT, GLD unless evaluation justifies a smaller broad-ETF basket.
- **Data source:** Alpaca/qfa real OHLCV only; no CSV-backed research.
- **Signal features:** AR-021 observed-session turn-of-month window plus 20/60-day realized volatility and simple SPY/TLT risk regime gate.
- **Model form:** qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` returning symbol weights.
- **Parameters / search space:** keep efficient; at most 2-3 prespecified volatility gates, no long grid search.
- **Position sizing:** Normalize gross exposure to 1.0 when active; allow flat state.
- **Risk constraints:** No live trading; no qfa daemon; include 5 bps turnover-cost haircut if qfa lacks native costs.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** Required, 6-12 random windows on Alpaca/qfa real data.
- **Metrics:** Sharpe, annualized return, annualized volatility, max drawdown, win rate, periods, cost/turnover proxy.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** Document native qfa support or ex-post haircut.
- **Orthogonality checks:** Compare retained return stream to AR-021 and AR-006 where available.
- **Acceptance threshold:** Positive median random-window Sharpe after costs and improved lower-tail drawdown versus AR-021.
