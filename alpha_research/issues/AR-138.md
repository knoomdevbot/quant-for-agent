## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Official SEC fails-to-deliver (FTD) publication files, used only after public release, may identify liquid U.S. securities with settlement-failure / short-sale crowding stress. Extreme normalized FTD after preceding price weakness may predict short-horizon rebound or squeeze/relief drift over the next 5-15 trading days.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: FTD spikes can reflect settlement pressure, hard-to-borrow stress, failed delivery, or crowded arbitrage/hedging. Once public and lagged conservatively, persistent high fails may mark names where borrow/settlement pressure forces covering or temporary distress mean-reverts.
- Expected failure mode: SEC FTD files are delayed and may be stale; CUSIP/ticker mapping may be fragile; FTD may just proxy distress, microcaps, corporate actions, or generic reversal; results may concentrate in meme-stock/crisis episodes.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Broad U.S.-listed common stocks and ETFs appearing in SEC FTD files with Alpaca/qfa daily bar coverage.
- Product-selection filters: unambiguous symbol mapping; Alpaca/qfa coverage; minimum price and median dollar volume; exclude OTC, preferreds, warrants, units, and illiquid microcaps where identifiable; no return-based selection.
- Selected universe: To be selected before scoring. Block/reject if fewer than 100 liquid mapped symbols, fewer than 500 events, or fewer than 5 years survive.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Uses a broad settlement-failure event source rather than OHLCV-only proxies. Limitations: official files are delayed and symbol/CUSIP mapping may be imperfect.
- Data source: SEC official FTD archive plus Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV. No CSV-backed market data, no qfa daemon, no orders.
- Signal features: FTD shares / recent volume, FTD dollar proxy / recent dollar volume, FTD persistence/change, lagged pre-publication return/volatility, liquidity masks.
- Model form: Long-only event allocator; primary 10d hold with 5d/15d diagnostics.
- Parameters / search space: fixed public-availability lag, extreme FTD percentile/z-score thresholds, pre-return weakness filter, per-symbol/date caps.
- Position sizing: equal-weight or volatility-scaled active events; cash when no events; no primary short book.
- Risk constraints: conservative publication timestamp; no trade before next session after public file availability; concentration caps by symbol/date/sector/year.

## Evaluation Plan
- Random-period protocol: First construct timestamp-safe event table; run no performance test if publication timing or mapping is unsafe. Then random-window and leave-one-year-out evaluation over SEC/qfa overlap.
- Metrics: median/p25/worst random-window Sharpe, positive-window rate, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover, activation/event count, event concentration.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: primary 10 bps one-way; sensitivities at 5 and 20 bps if feasible.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: compare against generic short-term reversal, AR-028/045/056 liquidity reversal, AR-103/119/121 liquidity-pressure failures, shifted SEC dates, randomized matched non-event dates, FTD-only and pre-return-only ablations.
- Acceptance threshold: positive median after-cost Sharpe, p25 not materially negative, positive-window rate >=55%, beats generic reversal/momentum and shifted-date controls, no 2020-2021/meme cluster dominance, max relevant correlation <=0.60.
