## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Public NOAA/CPC degree-day or temperature-anomaly releases, used only after timestamped public availability, identify weather-demand shocks that energy, natural-gas, utility, renewable, and related ETFs may under/overreact to over the next 1-10 trading days.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Heating/cooling degree-day surprises affect natural gas, electricity demand, utilities, and energy supply/demand expectations. A conservative post-release allocator can test whether public official weather-demand summaries still contain tradable delayed reaction after costs.
- **Expected failure mode:** Weather markets may price forecasts before official summaries; weekly NOAA summaries may be stale; effects may be seasonality/EIA/energy momentum in disguise; ETF proxies such as `UNG`/`USO` can have roll and cost frictions.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Broad weather-sensitive ETF pool considered ex ante: `UNG`, `USO`, `XLE`, `XOP`, `XLU`, `ICLN`, `TAN`, `KOL` if available, plus liquid substitutes/diagnostics such as `DBE`, `GSG`, `DBC`, `PDBC`, `XLRE`, `SPY` for beta controls.
- **Product-selection filters:** Alpaca/qfa daily bar availability, liquidity/volume proxy, economic exposure to weather-sensitive natural gas/energy/electricity/utilities/renewables, sufficient overlap with NOAA/CPC archives, no performance-based symbol selection.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed before performance by the research subagent after NOAA timestamp and Alpaca coverage checks.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Primary universe should emphasize clean weather-demand exposure and keep broad-market/commodity-beta ETFs as controls. Some ETFs may have short history, weak direct exposure, or roll/issuer effects.
- **Data source:** Official NOAA/CPC archived weekly degree-day or temperature-anomaly files with visible archive/release timestamps; use only after public availability. ETF prices/returns from Alpaca/qfa real daily market data only.
- **Signal features:** Heating/cooling degree-day anomaly versus rolling seasonal baseline, release-week demand shock sign/magnitude, season-specific energy/utility mapping, optional region/aggregate split if timestamp-safe.
- **Model form:** Rule-based event/weekly allocator with explicit parameters and conservative timestamp lag.
- **Parameters / search space:** Release lag next regular session or +1 business day if timezone ambiguous; holding period 1/3/5/10 trading days; anomaly lookback seasonal baseline 3-10 years if available; cost 10 bps primary.
- **Position sizing:** Capped long/short ETF sleeve weights or long/flat diagnostic if shorts are infeasible; per-symbol and gross exposure caps recorded.
- **Risk constraints:** No weather forecasts or price-inferred weather; no current annual snapshots treated as historical point-in-time unless archive timestamps prove availability; cap single ETF/sleeve concentration; document event count and seasonality concentration.
