## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamped SEC acceptance of registered common-stock offering prospectuses, prospectus supplements, registered direct offerings, secondary offerings, ATM/equity-distribution notices, or clearly linked offering 8-K disclosures may create short-horizon dilution/overhang or relief effects in liquid U.S. issuers after public EDGAR availability.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Equity issuance can change float, dilution expectations, supply overhang, balance-sheet runway, and investor positioning. Unlike deterministic filing-calendar attention, the event content has a direct financing/dilution mechanism.
- Expected failure mode: Form-type filters overcount noisy debt, notes, mixed shelves, preferreds, warrants, or boilerplate; parser cannot isolate common-stock offering terms; signal reduces to generic event drift, momentum/reversal, sector beta, or filing-calendar attention; small distressed issuers dominate.
- Falsifier: Reject before performance if a conservative timestamp-safe common-stock offering event table with >=150 liquid mapped events cannot be built. Reject after performance if matched non-events, shifted labels, generic filing drift, debt/prospectus form placebos, momentum/reversal, sector/beta controls, or issuer/year/month concentration dominate.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: SEC EDGAR offering-related forms over qfa/Alpaca overlap, including 424B5, 424B4, selected S-3/S-3ASR prospectus supplements, equity-distribution/ATM disclosures, registered direct/underwritten common-stock offerings, and linked 8-K offering disclosures.
- Product-selection filters: EDGAR acceptance timestamp present; issuer CIK maps conservatively to a liquid U.S. common stock with Alpaca bars; filing text parser classifies common-stock offering terms; excludes debt notes, preferreds, warrants, units, mixed shelf-only registrations, ETFs/funds, ADRs, OTC/illiquid names, and ambiguous cases.
- Selected universe: To be selected after source gate from liquid U.S. common stocks with reliable CIK/ticker mapping and validated common-stock offering events. Minimum gate: >=150 liquid mapped common-stock offering events with no issuer/year/month dominance.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Equity-offering events have a direct dilution/overhang mechanism, but SEC form metadata alone dramatically overstates usable events; conservative text parsing and exclusions are mandatory.
- Data source: SEC public company_tickers/submissions/filing documents for event source and EDGAR acceptance timestamps; qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV for returns only if source gate passes. No CSV market data, no daemon, no orders.
- Signal features: Offering type, common-stock share count/price/gross proceeds where parseable, primary vs secondary/ATM/registered-direct/underwritten labels, after-hours/public timestamp to next-session eligibility, liquidity/size filters, post-filing drift/reversal buckets.
- Model form: Source/event-table feasibility scout first; rule-based post-event drift/reversal event study only if source gate passes.
- Parameters / search space: 1/5/20 trading-day post-acceptance windows; short/underweight dilution-overhang candidates versus relief/reversal diagnostics; 10 bps primary one-way costs with 5/20 bps sensitivity.
- Position sizing: Event equal-weight or capped rank-weight portfolio, gross exposure <=1.0, max issuer/sector/date caps, cash when no valid events.
- Risk constraints: Trade only after EDGAR public acceptance time mapped to the next tradable qfa/Alpaca bar; after-hours filings enter no earlier than next regular session; no transaction-date inference; no form-type-only classification without text validation.
