## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Publicly known NYSE holidays and early-close sessions may concentrate investor de-risking/re-risking, liquidity effects, and calendar allocation flows; a strictly predeclared daily-bar rule may capture last-session-before-holiday broad equity ETF drift and/or first-session-after-holiday normalization.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Holiday periods can alter market participation and risk appetite in a predictable public calendar. Any edge should appear before or immediately after the holiday, not from lookahead event selection.
- Expected failure mode: The result is just turn-of-month, month-end, payday, OPEX, FOMC/CPI/payroll overlap, buy-and-hold equity beta, one holiday/year dominance, or a noisy sparse calendar artifact that fails matched placebo dates.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Broad liquid ETF pool with primary equity ETFs SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA; sector diagnostics XLB, XLC, XLE, XLF, XLI, XLK, XLP, XLRE, XLU, XLV, XLY; controls TLT, IEF, SHY, GLD.
- Product-selection filters: Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV coverage, liquidity, sufficient history, direct economic exposure to broad equity/sector risk; no return-based symbol selection.
- Selected universe: Primary decision sleeve should be SPY-only or a fixed equal-weight broad equity basket (SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA if covered). Sector ETFs are diagnostics unless predeclared.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Holiday risk-on/off drift is expected in broad equity risk first; sector diagnostics test breadth and concentration. Sparse annual holidays limit event count and raise data-mining risk.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV plus deterministic timestamp-safe NYSE holiday/early-close calendar rules. No CSV, no daemon, no orders.
- Signal features: Full NYSE holiday eve / last trading session before holiday, optional early-close holiday-eve flag if calendar support is deterministic, first trading session after holiday, overlap exclusions.
- Model form: Rule-based calendar-event allocator/event study.
- Parameters / search space: Pre-holiday sleeve only, post-holiday sleeve only, separated full-closure eve vs early-close eve, broad-basket vs SPY-only diagnostics; no return-optimized holiday selection.
- Position sizing: Fixed long broad equity exposure for predeclared event sleeves, optionally cash/SHY otherwise; diagnostics remain separate from primary unless predeclared.
- Risk constraints: Keep sleeves separate before any combined result; no leveraging; record event counts by holiday/year.

## Evaluation Plan
- Random-period protocol: Compact real-data daily replay over available history; event-study by known holiday dates; random/rolling windows with enough events; matched non-holiday same-weekday and within-year/month placebo dates with equal event count.
- Metrics: Median Sharpe, p25 Sharpe, worst-window Sharpe, positive-window rate, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover, event count, hit rate, placebo percentile, holiday/year attribution.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: 5 bps one-way primary with 10/20 bps sensitivity.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: Compare against AR-006/AR-021 turn-of-month, AR-110 payday/semi-monthly, AR-106 OPEX/rebalance, AR-123 Treasury payment-day, buy-and-hold SPY/equal-weight ETF basket, and existing watchlist equity/ETF allocation streams where available.
- Hard ablations / falsifiers: Matched non-holiday same-weekday placebo dates; randomized holiday labels within year/month; ex-turn-of-month and ex-month-end; ex-OPEX/third-Friday week and quarterly rebalance windows; ex-FOMC/CPI/payroll overlap diagnostics where deterministic calendars are available; holiday-by-holiday attribution; early-close-only vs full-closure-eve-only; pre-holiday vs post-holiday sleeves separated; SPY-only vs fixed basket; 5/10/20 bps costs.
- Acceptance threshold: At 10 bps, positive random-window median Sharpe, p25 not materially negative, positive-window rate >=55%, placebo percentile >=0.85, no single holiday/month/year dominance, 20 bps not fully collapsed, and max relevant proxy/library correlation <=0.60 hard / <=0.50 preferred.
