## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Economically equivalent or near-equivalent listed share classes of the same issuer can temporarily diverge due to share-class liquidity, index inclusion, voting/non-voting demand, and microstructure; lagged residual spread z-scores should mean revert over 1-10 trading days after costs.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Same-issuer share classes are a cleaner listed-equity relative-value relationship than generic stock pairs because cash flows and issuer fundamentals are largely shared, while liquidity/voting/index-demand premia can create temporary but bounded dislocations.
- Expected failure mode: Voting/liquidity premia may be persistent, pair count may be too small, lower-liquidity legs may be costly or hard to short, results may depend on one pair such as GOOGL/GOOG, or the signal may be a clone of generic pair stat-arb/liquidity reversal.

Exclude BRK.A/BRK.B if price level/data/size makes qfa evaluation impractical.
- Product-selection filters: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV coverage, both legs active over sufficient overlapping history, minimum dollar-volume/liquidity, comparable economic exposure, feasible price/sizing, no obvious corporate-action break in comparability, and no performance-based pair selection.
- Selected universe: To be fixed by evaluator before performance review from the candidate pool by coverage, liquidity, min-history, and economic-equivalence fit; pair count and exclusions must be documented.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Same-issuer pairs provide a cleaner economic anchor than generic equity stat-arb, but the universe is small and heterogeneous; borrow/short availability and voting-right premia are not directly observed by qfa daily bars.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no qfa daemon, no orders.
- Signal features: Rolling log price-ratio residual or spread z-score using 120d/252d histories; optional volatility normalization using only prior bars; thresholded long-cheap/short-rich weights within each pair.
- Model form: Rule-based pair relative-value/stat-arb allocator with pair-level dollar-neutral or volatility-normalized weights and pair caps.
- Parameters / search space: zscore_window=[120,252], entry_z=[1.0,1.5,2.0], exit_z=[0.25,0.5], holding/decay=[1,3,5,10 trading days], max_pair_weight, cost_bps_one_way=[5,10].
- Position sizing: Gross-normalized pair spreads; long cheap share class and short rich share class within each pair; equal pair caps; cash when fewer than minimum pairs/signals are active.
- Risk constraints: Reject if results depend on one pair, if 10 bps costs erase the edge, if random-window p25 Sharpe is materially negative, if pair-level contribution is not diversified enough, or if correlation to AR-005/AR-019/AR-045/AR-056 is high.
