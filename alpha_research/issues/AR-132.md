## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Fixed sponsor-and-exposure ETF clusters can show temporary residual-return dispersion after broad market stress; lagged residual outliers should partially converge over 3-5 trading days after realistic costs.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: The sponsor label is used only to define economically matched product groups, not as observed creation/redemption evidence. The test asks whether same-sponsor/exposure sleeves exhibit residual-dispersion convergence beyond generic ETF reversal, sector beta, and prior liquidity-pressure proxies.
- Expected failure mode: Signal collapses into simple ETF mean reversion / sector-style beta reversal, is dominated by transaction costs, or fails sponsor-label and exposure-matched placebos.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Broad US-listed liquid ETF universe from SPDR, iShares, and Vanguard equity broad-market/style/sector/factor sleeves with Alpaca/qfa daily OHLCV coverage.
- Product-selection filters: coverage since 2018 where possible, daily OHLCV availability, liquidity/volume screen, sponsor and exposure mapping available ex ante, avoid products with sparse history.
- Selected universe: To be selected before performance review from SPDR/iShares/Vanguard sponsor/exposure clusters; record symbols, cluster mapping, coverage, and exclusions.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Prefer broad enough sponsor/exposure clusters to test whether sponsor grouping adds value; if coverage collapses to a narrow basket, mark the result limited or rejected rather than force a conclusion.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no daemon, no orders.
- Signal features: Lagged daily returns, rolling residuals of sleeve ETFs versus broad-market and exposure proxies, within-cluster residual-dispersion z-score, stress/dispersion gate. No primary abnormal volume, close-location, range, gap/close residual, or liquidity-pressure composite.
- Model form: Rule-based residual-dispersion mean-reversion allocator.
- Parameters / search space: residual lookback 60/126/252 trading days; dispersion z threshold 1.0/1.5/2.0; holding period 3/5 days; stress gate on broad-market volatility or return drawdown defined using past bars only.
- Position sizing: Dollar-neutral or beta-aware fade of residual outliers within cluster with gross exposure cap; normalize weights.
- Risk constraints: Max per-symbol weight cap, cluster exposure caps, turnover/cost tracking, next-bar or next-close timestamp safety.

## Evaluation Plan
- Random-period protocol: Multiple random windows over 2018-present where coverage permits, with smoke test first; evaluate distribution rather than one period.
- Metrics: Sharpe, Sortino if available, annualized return, annualized volatility, max drawdown, turnover, hit rate, positive-window rate, activation rate, cost sensitivity.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: 10 bps primary; 20 bps sensitivity/collapse gate.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: Correlation of candidate daily returns/equity proxies versus accepted/watchlist alphas where available; especially AR-015, AR-037, AR-039, AR-045/056, AR-063 and related ETF streams. Mark deferred_due_rejection if decisive rejection occurs before expensive orthogonality.
- Required ablations/placebos: AR-121 family-relative liquidity-pressure proxy, AR-103 close-volume dislocation proxy, AR-075/098 sector dispersion, AR-116 breadth/concentration, AR-015 TSMOM, simple ETF reversal/mean reversion, sector/style beta reversal, sponsor-label placebo, exposure-matched placebo, no-stress-gate variant, no-sponsor-cluster variant.
- Acceptance threshold: Positive 10 bps median random-window Sharpe, p25 not materially negative, positive-window rate >=55%, tolerable max drawdown/turnover, no 20 bps cost collapse, max relevant correlation <=0.60, and must beat placebos/ablations.
