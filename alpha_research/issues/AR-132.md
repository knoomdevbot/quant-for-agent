## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Known Treasury coupon/principal payment dates may create mechanical reinvestment demand in direct Treasury duration ETFs, producing a close-to-close drift on payment dates.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Treasury cashflows are scheduled and public. Reinvestment demand from coupon/principal payments may concentrate in duration exposures around payment dates, but the effect must be separable from generic turn-of-month, month-end, rates momentum, and macro-event drift.
- **Expected failure mode:** The effect is only generic turn-of-month/month-end seasonality, unconditional duration beta, TLT/IEF momentum or reversal, FOMC/CPI/payroll/auction/refunding overlap, one rates regime/year, or a repackaged failed fixed-income calendar-flow idea.

## Implementation Plan
- **Primary event definition:** Deterministic, public, timestamp-safe Treasury coupon/principal payment dates only. The research subagent must encode the calendar before performance review and block/reject if exact public payment dates cannot be encoded reliably without post-hoc return inspection.
- **Primary leg predeclared before performance review:** Prior close to payment-date close, long equal-weight direct Treasury duration basket. Payment-date close-to-next-close and minus-one-to-plus-one windows are diagnostics only and cannot rescue a failed primary leg.
- **Candidate product pool:** Direct Treasury duration/inflation ETFs selected ex ante from `TLT`, `IEF`, `SHY`, `GOVT`, `TIP`; controls may include `LQD`, `HYG`, `SPY`, `QQQ`, and cash/short-duration proxies but must not enter the primary basket unless justified before performance review.
- **Product-selection filters:** Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV coverage, sufficient history over the event sample, liquidity/dollar-volume proxy, direct economic exposure to Treasury duration or inflation-linked Treasuries, and no return-ranked inclusion.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed by the research subagent before performance review from the candidate pool. Report selected/excluded symbols with coverage, liquidity, exposure rationale, and limitations.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Daily ETF bars can test tradable close-to-close calendar effects but cannot observe actual reinvestment flows, Treasury cashflow magnitudes, dealer inventory, or intraday execution. LQD/HYG are controls only because they add credit beta.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV plus deterministic public Treasury payment calendar logic. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** Payment-date flag, holiday adjustment flag, day-of-month/month-end/turn-of-month labels, overlap flags for FOMC/CPI/payroll/Treasury auctions/refunding when feasible, prior close to payment-date close primary return, diagnostic windows, event-year labels, and duration momentum/reversal controls.
- **Model form:** Rule-based event-calendar allocator with cash/zero exposure outside event windows; no optimizer and no flow-magnitude conditioning.
- **Parameters / search space:** Primary event calendar, primary leg, and selected universe fixed before performance review. Primary cost: 10 bps one-way with 5 and 20 bps sensitivity. Diagnostics cannot rescue failed primary.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-weight fixed primary Treasury ETF basket, gross exposure <= 1.0, no leverage. Report sleeve/product contributions without product mining.
- **Risk constraints:** Reject if performance is explained by same-day-of-month, turn-of-month/month-end, unconditional TLT/IEF exposure, duration momentum/reversal, macro/auction overlaps, one regime/year, or post-hoc diagnostic leg selection.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** qfa/Alpaca smoke plus full event study over all reliably encoded payment dates; leave-one-year-out; same-day-of-month placebo; random matched non-event dates; ex-turn-of-month and ex-month-end subsets; overlap-exclusion subsets for FOMC/CPI/payroll/Treasury auction/refunding where feasible.
- **Metrics:** Median/mean/p25/worst event return, event Sharpe where meaningful, hit rate, positive-year rate, max event-equity drawdown, turnover, activation/event count, cost sensitivity, single-year contribution/dominance, and product contribution diagnostics.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 10 bps one-way primary, with 5 and 20 bps stress.
- **Required controls / ablations:** Same-day-of-month placebo, random non-event placebo, ex-ToM/ex-month-end, duration momentum and reversal proxies, unconditional TLT/IEF event-window exposure, `LQD`/`HYG` control behavior, and comparisons against AR-006/AR-021 turn-of-month, AR-108 Treasury auction, AR-114 municipal reinvestment, AR-125 month-end rebalance, and recent scheduled-event failure AR-130.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** Compare against compact accepted/watchlist streams if available for AR-006/AR-021 ToM, AR-015 ETF TSMOM, AR-037/049 carry/defensive, and simple duration proxy streams. If compact comparable streams are unavailable, mark explicitly and do not use orthogonality to rescue weak performance.
- **Acceptance threshold:** At 10 bps, median event return must be > 0, p25 not materially negative, hit rate >= 55%, placebo rank >= 0.85, positive-year profile not dominated by one regime/year, and ex-ToM/ex-month-end exclusions must not destroy the result. Reject if the primary leg fails and diagnostics are needed to rescue it.
