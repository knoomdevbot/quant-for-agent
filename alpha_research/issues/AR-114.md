## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Municipal bond ETFs may exhibit temporary tax-sensitive flow pressure and reversal around predeclared late-November/December tax-loss selling, April tax-payment liquidity, and January reinvestment/coupon-demand windows.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Municipal bond ETF holders are more tax-sensitive than generic Treasury ETF holders. Calendar-driven selling or reinvestment pressure can create temporary dislocations not fully captured by generic duration carry, Treasury auction supply, or turn-of-month seasonality.
- Expected failure mode: Signal is mostly duration/credit beta, turn-of-month/month-end seasonality, or too low-volatility for costs; universe is too narrow or sparse after liquidity/history filters.

- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only; no CSV; no daemon; no orders.

- Model form: Rule-based calendar-flow allocator with SHY/cash fallback when no muni sleeve qualifies.
- Parameters / search space: Predeclared tax/reinvestment windows only; weekly or event-hold rebalance; 2/5/10 bps cost sensitivity; no performance-selected date mining.

- Risk constraints: Reject if fallback dominates returns, if one symbol/window/year dominates, or if duration/credit beta explains the result.
