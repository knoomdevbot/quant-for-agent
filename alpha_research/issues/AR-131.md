## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Publicly scheduled weekly EIA Petroleum Status Report releases may create a tradable close-to-close drift or fade in oil/energy ETFs as inventory-risk hedging and post-release rebalancing concentrate in energy exposures.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** The release calendar is known ex ante and may concentrate risk transfer in oil, exploration/production, services, broad energy, and commodity ETFs. A fixed release-day rule could capture scheduled inventory-risk premia without using realized inventory-surprise magnitude.
- **Expected failure mode:** The effect is just Wednesday/day-of-week seasonality, generic energy beta, crude/energy TSMOM or short-term reversal, holiday-shift artifacts, a few oil-regime years, or another failed scheduled-event drift/fade pattern like AR-130.

## Implementation Plan
- **Primary event definition:** Public scheduled weekly EIA Petroleum Status Report release dates only, including deterministic holiday-shifted releases when documented before performance review. Do not use realized inventory changes, surprise magnitude, revisions, API data, headlines, or other post-release information.
- **Primary leg predeclared before performance review:** Prior close to EIA release-day close, equal-weight long fixed selected energy/oil ETF basket. Post-release next-day fade/continuation and release-day-to-plus-two/plus-three windows are diagnostics only unless separately preflighted after the primary result.
- **Candidate product pool:** Energy/oil/commodity ETFs selected ex ante from `XLE`, `VDE`, `IYE`, `XOP`, `OIH`, `XES`, `USO`, `BNO`, `DBC`, `DBA`; controls may include `SPY`, `QQQ`, `GLD`, `TLT`, and simple energy beta proxies but must not be included in the primary basket unless economically justified before performance review.
- **Product-selection filters:** Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV coverage, sufficient history over the event sample, liquidity/dollar-volume proxy, direct economic exposure to broad energy, E&P, oil services, crude oil, or broad commodity beta, and no return-ranked inclusion.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed by the research subagent before performance review from the candidate pool. Report selected and excluded symbols with coverage, liquidity, and exposure rationale.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Daily ETF bars can test tradable close-to-close scheduled-release effects, but cannot observe intraday 10:30 ET reaction, inventory-surprise magnitude, futures curve positioning, or order flow. The hypothesis must remain a price-only public-calendar event study.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV plus a timestamp-safe public EIA scheduled-release calendar. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** EIA release-day flag, holiday-shift indicator, weekday/month labels, prior close to release-day close primary return, post-release diagnostic returns, event-year labels, and overlap flags for FOMC/CPI/payroll/OPEX/turn-of-month/month-end when feasible.
- **Model form:** Rule-based event-calendar allocator with cash/zero exposure outside event windows; no optimizer and no surprise-conditioned weights.
- **Parameters / search space:** Primary event leg and selected universe fixed before performance review. Primary cost: 10 bps one-way with 5 and 20 bps sensitivity. Diagnostics may inspect next-day and two/three-day post-release windows, but cannot rescue a failed primary leg.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-weight fixed basket over selected ETFs, gross exposure <= 1.0, no leverage. Report sleeve/product contributions without product mining.
- **Risk constraints:** Reject if performance is explained by same-weekday seasonality, generic energy beta, energy/crude TSMOM or reversal, holiday-shifted dates, one oil regime/year, or post-hoc diagnostic leg selection.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** qfa/Alpaca smoke plus full weekly EIA scheduled-event study over all available coverage; leave-one-year-out; same-month/same-weekday placebo; random matched non-EIA release dates; holiday-shift diagnostics; and overlap-exclusion subsets.
- **Metrics:** Median/mean/p25/worst event return, event Sharpe where meaningful, hit rate, positive-year rate, max event-equity drawdown, turnover, activation/event count, cost sensitivity, single-year contribution/dominance, and product contribution diagnostics.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 10 bps one-way primary, with 5 and 20 bps stress.
- **Required controls / ablations:** Same-weekday/same-month placebo, random non-event placebo, simple Wednesday energy exposure, `XLE`/`USO`/`DBC` momentum and reversal proxies, broad SPY/QQQ risk beta, crude/commodity beta where feasible through ETF proxies, holiday-shift exclusion, ex-FOMC/CPI/payroll/OPEX/ToM/month-end subsets where feasible, and comparison against recent scheduled-event failures AR-127/AR-129/AR-130.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** Compare against compact accepted/watchlist streams if available for AR-015 ETF TSMOM, AR-037/049 carry/defensive, AR-043 liquidity stress, AR-127 holiday risk premium, and simple energy/broad-market proxy streams. If compact comparable event returns are unavailable, mark explicitly and do not use orthogonality to rescue weak performance.
- **Acceptance threshold:** At 10 bps, median event return must be > 0, p25 not materially negative, hit rate >= 55%, placebo rank >= 0.85, positive-year profile not dominated by one regime/year, holiday-shift exclusion must not destroy the result, and energy momentum/reversal/beta controls must not explain the return stream. Reject if the primary leg fails and diagnostics are needed to rescue it.
