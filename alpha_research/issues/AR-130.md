## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Scheduled FOMC decision days may create a timestamp-safe short-horizon drift or fade in rate-sensitive ETFs due to public-calendar hedging, risk-budgeting, and rebalance flows. The effect should be testable as a narrow scheduled-event calendar anomaly without using realized macro surprise magnitude.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** FOMC decision dates are known ex ante and concentrate positioning, hedging, and post-announcement rebalancing in duration, credit, gold/USD, equity, financial, real-estate, and utility exposures. A fixed event-calendar rule may capture pre-event drift or post-event normalization distinct from generic duration carry, TSMOM, month-end, OPEX, payday, Treasury auction-week, or real-rate shock-fade effects.
- **Expected failure mode:** The apparent edge is just generic duration/risk-off beta, ETF TSMOM, month-end/ToM/OPEX/payday exposure, Treasury auction overlap, single-year rate-regime dependence, post-hoc leg selection, or a repeat of rejected AR-070/AR-078/AR-108/AR-109 behavior.

## Implementation Plan
- **Primary event definition:** Scheduled FOMC decision days only, using hardcoded public Fed meeting-calendar decision dates. Exclude CPI, payroll, PPI, unscheduled emergency meetings, and any primary conditioning on realized macro surprise/shock magnitude.
- **Primary leg predeclared before performance review:** Prior-close to FOMC decision-day close drift leg on the fixed selected ETF basket. Post-decision 1-day fade and post-decision 2-3 day continuation/fade are diagnostics only unless separately preflighted after the primary result.
- **Candidate product pool:** Core rate-sensitive ETFs selected ex ante: TLT, IEF, SHY, LQD, HYG, GLD, SPY, QQQ, XLF, XLRE. Optional TIP, UUP, and XLU may be added only if Alpaca/qfa coverage and liquidity are clean and inclusion is documented before performance review.
- **Product-selection filters:** Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV coverage, liquidity/dollar-volume proxy, direct economic exposure to duration, curve/cash, credit, gold/USD, broad equity, financials, real estate, or utilities, and no return-ranked product inclusion.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed by the research subagent before performance review from the candidate pool. Report selected and excluded symbols with coverage/liquidity rationale.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** ETF daily bars can test tradable close-to-close calendar effects but cannot observe FOMC intraday statement/press-conference reactions, fed funds futures surprises, positioning, or order flow. The hypothesis must remain a price-only public-calendar event study, not a macro-surprise study.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV plus hardcoded public scheduled FOMC decision dates from Fed calendars. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** Scheduled FOMC decision-day flag, prior-close to decision-day close primary drift window, diagnostic decision-day-close to next-close fade, diagnostic decision-day-close to +2/+3 trading-day continuation/fade, event-year labels, month/week/day-of-week labels, and overlap flags for ToM/month-end/OPEX/payday/Treasury auction-week.
- **Model form:** Rule-based event-calendar allocator with cash/zero exposure outside event windows; no optimizer.
- **Parameters / search space:** Primary leg and selected universe fixed before performance review. Diagnostic legs: post-decision 1 trading day and post-decision 2-3 trading days. Primary cost: 10 bps one-way with 5 and 20 bps sensitivity.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-weight or equal-risk capped basket over selected ETFs, gross exposure <= 1.0, no leverage. If long/short fade diagnostics use realized ETF event-day returns, they must be labeled diagnostics and must not rescue a failed primary drift leg.
- **Risk constraints:** Reject if the result depends on one year/rate regime, calendar overlaps, post-hoc leg choice, or generic duration/risk-off/TSMOM/month-end exposure.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** qfa/Alpaca smoke plus full scheduled-FOMC event study over all available coverage; leave-one-year-out; same-month/same-weekday placebo dates; random matched non-FOMC event dates; and overlap-exclusion subsets.
- **Metrics:** Median/mean/p25/worst event return, event Sharpe where meaningful, hit rate, positive-year rate, positive-event rate, max event-equity drawdown, turnover, activation/event count, cost sensitivity, and single-year contribution.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 10 bps one-way primary, with 5 and 20 bps sensitivity.
- **Required controls / ablations:** Same-month/same-weekday placebo, ex-turn-of-month, ex-month-end, ex-OPEX, ex-payday, ex-Treasury-auction-week, unconditional same-window exposure, ETF TSMOM AR-015 proxy, month-end AR-125 proxy, auction-week AR-108 proxy, real-rate shock-fade AR-109 proxy, FOMC/CPI event-shock AR-070 conceptual control, intraday announcement AR-078 conceptual control, and duration/risk-off/carry simple proxies.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** AR-070, AR-078, AR-108, AR-109, AR-125, AR-129, AR-015, plus simple TLT/IEF/SHY duration, SPY/QQQ equity, GLD/UUP, and credit-risk proxy streams where compact aligned returns are available. If unavailable, report explicitly and do not use orthogonality to rescue weak performance.
- **Acceptance threshold:** At 10 bps, median event return must be > 0, hit rate >= 55%, placebo rank >= 0.85, p25 not materially negative, positive-year rate not dependent on one year/regime, ex-calendar-overlap subset must retain positive median, and max relevant correlation <= 0.60 hard / <= 0.50 preferred. Reject if performance disappears after excluding ToM/month-end/OPEX/payday/auction-week overlaps or if high correlation to TSMOM/month-end/risk-off/duration carry/shock-fade proxies explains returns.
