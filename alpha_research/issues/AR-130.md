## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Around deterministic SEC Form 13F quarterly filing-deadline dates, widely watched liquid mega-cap/common-stock names that show abnormal pre-deadline volume and sector-residual price pressure may experience short-horizon post-deadline residual reversal as attention/copycat demand and disclosure-window positioning normalize.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: 13F deadlines are public, timestamp-safe information-attention events. Even without using filing contents, the filing deadline can concentrate media/investor attention on large institutional holdings and crowded mega-cap names. If attention/positioning pressure is visible in completed OHLCV before or on the deadline, the post-deadline leg can be tested as a mechanical crowding/attention-normalization effect rather than an earnings, dividend, split, OPEX, holiday, month-end, Treasury, EIA, FOMC, or Russell effect.
- Expected failure mode: Effect is generic close-volume/liquidity reversal already captured by AR-028/045/056; event count is sparse or dominated by one year/quarter; static universe survivorship bias overwhelms inference; pre-deadline pressure is just momentum/reversal, sector beta, earnings contamination, or market regime; costs and turnover erase a low-capacity single-name signal.

- Product-selection filters: Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV availability; sufficient daily history over 2020-present or broader available period; high median dollar volume and common-stock primary sleeve where feasible; sector proxy mappability; no return/performance-based symbol selection.
- Selected universe: To be fixed by evaluator before performance review using only coverage, liquidity, common-stock classification, and sector-mapping filters. Record selected symbols and limitations.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: The mechanism requires large, widely held names plausibly affected by disclosure-window attention. Static current large-cap membership is acceptable only as a fast-falsification approximation and must be labeled survivorship-biased.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real market OHLCV only, plus deterministic public SEC 13F deadline calendar: 45 calendar days after quarter-end, shifted to the next business/trading day when needed. No CSV; no `--data-csv`; no daemon; no orders.
- Signal features: Deterministic 13F-deadline event flag; pre-deadline 3/5/10 trading-day abnormal dollar-volume z-score; pre-deadline sector-residual return pressure versus mapped sector ETF and SPY; close-location/range pressure diagnostics only as controls, not primary source of edge; optional post-deadline 1-day confirmation only if timestamp-safe and predeclared.
- Model form: Rule-based event-calendar residual reversal. After a completed 13F-deadline session, short/underweight names with positive abnormal residual pressure and long/overweight names with negative abnormal residual pressure, sector-capped and beta/market neutral where feasible. Cash/zero weights when too few names qualify.
- Parameters / search space: pre_deadline_windows=[3,5,10]; volume_z_lookbacks=[60,120]; residual_pressure_thresholds=[top/bottom decile,top/bottom quintile]; holding_periods=[2,3,5]; cost_bps_one_way=[5,10,20].
- Position sizing: Equal-weight or inverse-volatility within long/short sleeves; gross exposure normalized; single-name and sector caps; optional dollar-neutral/beta-neutral diagnostic variant.
- Risk constraints: Reject if fewer than a minimum number of independent deadline events/windows; reject if one quarter/year dominates; reject if generic residual reversal or close-volume reversal explains results; record survivorship and earnings-contamination limitations.
