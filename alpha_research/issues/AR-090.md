## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Rising Google Trends attention for consumer categories/brands can lead or confirm short-horizon flows into discretionary, retail, travel, and broad risk ETFs.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Retail search attention is a noisy but timely proxy for consumer interest and narrative attention; changes may precede flows/returns in XLY, XRT, JETS/PEJ-like proxies if supported, QQQ/SPY, or defensive rotation away from them.
- **Expected failure mode:** Google Trends is normalized/revised, category mapping is unstable, attention is already priced, or the ETF universe is too coarse for search terms.
- **Falsifier:** Median random-window Sharpe <= 0 after costs, p25 Sharpe materially negative, unstable across random windows, unavailable external data with timestamp discipline, or high redundancy with existing ETF momentum/defensive alphas.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Consumer discretionary/staples/retail/travel/e-commerce ETFs plus liquid constituent equities and adjacent risk/defensive ETFs. Start from broad product families such as XLY/XLP/XRT/PEJ/JETS/IYT/ITB/KRE/QQQ/SPY/IWM/SHY/GLD where data is available, then add 50-150 liquid exposed equities across retail, travel, restaurants, autos, housing, luxury, e-commerce, payments, and staples.
- **Product-selection filters:** Real-data availability, dollar-volume/liquidity, direct mapping to predeclared Google Trends query baskets, sector/theme purity, and avoidance of near-duplicate exposures.
- **Selected universe target:** 30-80 products after filtering; a smaller pilot is allowed only if Trends or qfa data access is limiting and must be marked preliminary.
- **Seed universe examples:** XLY, XRT, SPY, QQQ, XLP, XLU, SHY, GLD, IWM, XLE, plus idea-mapped liquid consumer equities.
- **External signal source:** Google Trends via pytrends/SerpAPI/Trends export if available plus Alpaca/qfa real OHLCV; no CSV-backed market-price research. If Trends access is unavailable, mark blocked rather than substituting fake data.
- **Signal features:** Weekly/daily search-interest changes for baskets such as “buy car”, “travel”, “restaurant”, “luxury”, “retail sales”, brand/sector proxies; cross-sectional attention momentum; volatility/risk filter from ETF prices.
- **Model form:** Rule-based qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` returning symbol weights; external signal ingestion must be deterministic, cached only as compact derived features if allowed, and documented.
- **Parameters / search space:** attention z-score lookback 26/52 weeks, attention change horizon 1/4 weeks, price confirmation 20/60 days, rebalance weekly, max ETF weight 0.35.
- **Position sizing:** Gross exposure <= 1.0, no leverage, max single ETF/stock weight per config, SHY/cash-like fallback allowed when signal confidence is low.
- **Risk constraints:** No daemon, no orders, no raw paid/vendor data committed, no secrets, no lookahead from revised external data. If external data cannot be obtained reproducibly, mark blocked rather than fabricating a proxy.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** External-signal availability check, qfa/Alpaca smoke test, then 6-12 random/stress windows using strictly point-in-time or lagged external features.
- **Metrics:** Median/p25/worst Sharpe after costs, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover proxy, win rate, positive-window rate, activation rate.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** Minimum 5-10 bps one-way turnover-cost proxy; higher stress if signal changes frequently or uses less liquid ETFs.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** Compare against AR-003/015 ETF TSMOM, AR-028/045 closing-volume reversal, AR-037/049 defensive carry, AR-051/063 stress/carry variants, and recent cross-asset ETF alphas where compact returns exist.
- **Acceptance threshold:** Positive median and acceptable p25 random-window Sharpe after costs, low redundancy, reproducible external-signal pipeline, and no obvious timestamp leakage.
