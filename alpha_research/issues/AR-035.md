## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Liquid ETFs may earn better risk-adjusted returns by rotating exposure based on cross-asset realized-volatility regime and defensive/risk-on breadth, rather than trading overnight gap reversal/continuation.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** This uses a different return driver from AR-012: regime allocation and volatility-risk budgeting instead of opening-imbalance microstructure.
- **Expected failure mode:** Volatility regime signal lags turning points, underperforms buy-and-hold in risk-on markets, or is redundant with existing risk-off switch alphas.
- **Falsifier:** Median random-period Sharpe <= 0 after documented/applied costs, or high redundancy with stronger watchlist regime alphas without improvement.

## Implementation Plan
- **Universe:** SPY,QQQ,IWM,TLT,GLD,SLV,XLF,XLK,XLE,XLV
- **Data source:** Alpaca/qfa real OHLCV only; no CSV-backed research.
- **Signal features:** realized volatility, cross-asset breadth, SPY/TLT/GLD defensive alignment, trailing drawdown/risk-on filter.
- **Model form:** Rule-based qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` returning symbol weights.
- **Parameters / search space:** vol_window=[20,60], breadth_window=[20,60], risk_budget=[0.5,1.0], defensive_weight=[0.25,0.5,0.75].

- **Metrics:** Sharpe, annualized return, annualized volatility, max drawdown, win rate, periods, turnover/cost proxy.

- **Acceptance threshold:** Positive median cost-adjusted random-window Sharpe, tolerable drawdown, and non-redundant versus existing watchlist alphas.
