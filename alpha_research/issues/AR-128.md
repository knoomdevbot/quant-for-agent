## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** Proximity to a lagged 52-week high/low captures investor anchoring and attention that is not fully explained by raw 12-1 momentum. Confirmed breakouts near highs may continue, while near-low loser rebounds should be diagnostic only unless stabilized and non-redundant.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Investors anchor on salient 52-week high/low levels; new highs can attract attention and trend-following while extreme lows can reflect overreaction only after stabilization. The signal should be distinguishable from simple momentum if it adds incremental information beyond trailing returns.
- **Expected failure mode:** The signal is just AR-001-style cross-sectional momentum, AR-015 ETF TSMOM, simple beta/sector/volatility exposure, or another failed breakout/reversal clone; static coverage introduces survivorship limitations; ETF sleeve is dominated by TSMOM.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Broad liquid large-cap equities and liquid ETFs across broad index, sectors, styles, bonds/credit, commodities, real assets, and defensive/cash-like sleeves.
- **Product-selection filters:** Alpaca/qfa daily OHLCV coverage, liquidity/dollar-volume proxy, sufficient lagged 252-day history, stable symbol coverage; no current-index membership claims and no return-based inclusion except the predeclared signal ranks.
- **Selected universe:** To be fixed before performance review. Equities and ETFs must be evaluated separately before any combined model.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** A 52-week anchoring effect may be stronger in equities than ETFs; ETF sleeve is a redundancy stress test against AR-015. Static qfa/Alpaca coverage reduces implementation complexity but may introduce survivorship and delisting bias.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only. No CSV, no `--data-csv`, no daemon, no orders.
- **Signal features:** Lagged rolling 252-trading-day high/low proximity, confirmed breakout flag, 12-1 or 126-day momentum control, volatility/beta/sector diagnostics, stabilization filter for near-low diagnostics. Rolling extrema must be shifted at least one bar.
- **Model form:** Rule-based monthly cross-sectional ranker. Primary model: long near-high confirmed breakouts and avoid/underweight near-lows. Loser rebound is a separate diagnostic sleeve only, not a post-hoc rescue path.
- **Parameters / search space:** Primary monthly rebalance; lagged 252-day high/low; predeclared top/bottom quantiles or capped basket sizes; diagnostics for proximity-only, breakout-only, momentum-only, stabilization-only, and 20 bps stress.
- **Position sizing:** Equal-weight or volatility-scaled capped long/underweight sleeves; no opaque optimizer.
- **Risk constraints:** 10 bps primary one-way cost; 20 bps stress; reject if incremental performance disappears vs simple momentum/TSMOM or if correlations exceed hard redundancy gates.

## Evaluation Plan

- **Metrics:** Median/mean/p25/worst random-window Sharpe, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover, hit rate, activation, positive-window rate, sleeve attribution, cost sensitivity.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 10 bps one-way primary, 20 bps stress where feasible.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** Compare conceptually/numerically where compact streams exist against AR-001 cross-sectional momentum, AR-015 ETF TSMOM, AR-028/AR-045/AR-056 reversal/liquidity, AR-004/AR-057 breakout failures, simple 12-1 momentum, beta/sector/volatility controls.
- **Acceptance threshold:** Positive incremental 10 bps median and p25 random-window Sharpe after simple momentum/ETF TSMOM controls, positive-window rate above ~55%, max relevant correlation <=0.60 hard (<=0.50 preferred), and no result explained by market beta, sector concentration, volatility timing, or a simple momentum clone.
