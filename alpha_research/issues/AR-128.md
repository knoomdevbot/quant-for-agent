## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Large same-day sector ETF residual shocks versus SPY may be absorbed by sector constituents unevenly. Liquid large-cap constituents that lag the mapped sector ETF shock, after controlling for their own recent return and market beta proxy, may catch up over the next 1-3 sessions.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Sector ETF/index-flow and basket-rebalancing pressure can move the liquid sector proxy first, while individual constituents with heterogeneous investor attention or idiosyncratic noise may adjust with a delay. This is a cross-sectional lead-lag/catch-up test, not a broad momentum, volume/liquidity, calendar, carry/defensive, or ETF allocation rule.
- Expected failure mode: Signal collapses into simple single-name momentum/reversal, sector ETF continuation/reversal, mega-cap tech concentration, or a generic liquidity/close-location effect; turnover/costs erase the edge; static constituent mapping creates survivorship limitations.

## Implementation Plan

SPY/QQQ/IWM are controls only unless explicitly used as diagnostics.
- Product-selection filters: Alpaca/qfa real daily OHLCV availability; sufficient history across the random-window evaluator; high median dollar-volume and minimum price filters; static sector mapping fixed before performance review; common-stock primary sleeve where feasible; exclude ETFs/funds/REITs from traded single-name sleeve; no return-based universe selection.
- Selected universe: To be fixed by evaluator before performance review using only coverage, liquidity, common-stock classification, and sector-mapping filters. Record selected symbols, sector map, final universe size, and limitations.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: The mechanism requires sector-level proxy shocks and liquid constituent catch-up candidates. Static current large-cap membership is acceptable only as a fast-falsification approximation and must be labeled survivorship-biased.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only; no CSV; no `--data-csv`; no daemon; no orders.
- Signal features: Lagged 1-day sector ETF residual return versus SPY; constituent same-day residual return versus SPY and mapped sector ETF; underreaction score defined as sector ETF residual shock minus constituent sector-residual move; optional 3-5 day constituent recent-return control to avoid pure reversal/momentum; realized-volatility scaling and sector caps.
- Model form: Rule-based cross-sectional ranker. After a sector ETF residual shock at close t, enter at next rebalance using only lagged daily bars. Long constituents that underreacted to positive sector shocks and/or short or underweight those that overreacted, with a separately reported long-only version if shorting feasibility is a concern.
- Parameters / search space: shock_lookback=[1,3]; residualization=[SPY_only, SPY_plus_sector]; holding_period_days=[1,2,3]; top_bottom_fraction=[0.15,0.20,0.25]; sector_caps=[0.20,0.25]; cost_bps_one_way=[5,10,20].
- Position sizing: Equal-weight or volatility-scaled constituent basket, gross exposure normalized, sector caps enforced, cash/no-trade if too few qualified names. No leverage beyond normalized model weights.
- Risk constraints: Primary cost gate at 10 bps one-way; record turnover and activation; reject if one sector, one symbol cluster, or one regime dominates; sector ETFs are signal/context, not the primary traded sleeve.
