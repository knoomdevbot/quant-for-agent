## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Mega-cap stocks that show unusual idiosyncratic range/volatility expansion without same-day follow-through tend to normalize over the next 3–10 trading days after removing broad-market and sector context.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Sudden single-name stress can temporarily exhaust liquidity and risk limits. If the move is mostly idiosyncratic and does not close with continuation pressure, liquidity providers and systematic rebalancers may fade the residual stress.
- Expected failure mode: The signal may be a clone of prior close-location/liquidity reversal alphas, may be dominated by a few high-volatility mega-cap names, may be erased by 10–20 bps costs, or may actually capture market/sector rebound beta rather than idiosyncratic normalization.
