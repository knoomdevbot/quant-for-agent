## Alpha Hypothesis
- **Claim:** A predeclared mid-month payday/contribution-flow event window around the first tradable session on or after the 16th calendar day produces a positive, cost-resilient short-horizon equity ETF return that is distinct from standard turn-of-month and option-expiry/rebalance-week effects.
- **Economic / behavioral intuition:** Semi-monthly payroll and retirement-account contribution flows may create predictable incremental buying pressure in broad equity index funds after the 15th. Quantpedia cites evidence that the 16th calendar day ranks unusually well versus other month days.
- **Expected failure mode:** The effect is too small after realistic transaction costs, is only turn-of-month/OPEX seasonality in disguise, is concentrated in a few historical decades/years, or is data-mined by trying many nearby days/hold lengths/ETFs.
- **Falsifier:** 10 bps random-window median Sharpe <= 0, materially negative p25 Sharpe, loss of return after ex-ToM and ex-OPEX controls, performance concentrated in a single year/regime, or max relevant correlation > 0.60 to AR-006/AR-021/AR-041/AR-106/proxy calendar returns.

## Implementation Plan
- **Candidate product pool:** Primary SPY/S&P 500 proxy; diagnostic breadth only across broad/sector/style ETFs such as SPY, VOO/VTI if available, QQQ, IWM, DIA, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLI, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLB, XLRE, IWF, IWD, MTUM, VLUE, QUAL, USMV.
- **Product-selection filters:** History coverage, liquidity/dollar-volume proxy, clean Alpaca/qfa daily bars, and no return-ranked ETF selection.
- **Selected universe:** SPY primary; ETF panel only for mechanism breadth and robustness diagnostics.
- **Universe-selection rationale / limitations:** Daily OHLCV and public calendar data can test the calendar-return pattern but cannot observe payroll, retirement contributions, fund flows, or intraday execution.
- **Data source:** qfa/Alpaca real market data only; no CSV-backed research.
- **Signal features:** First tradable session on/after the 16th calendar day, 1-day primary hold, optional 2-day secondary hold, explicit exclusions/overlap counts for turn-of-month and OPEX/third-Friday weeks, optional volume/breadth confirmation as an ablation only.
- **Model form:** Rule-based qfa-compatible `generate_signals(context)` calendar-event allocator.
- **Parameters / search space:** Primary event day fixed at calendar-day 16; secondary variants limited to 2-day hold and 16th trading-day diagnostic; 5/10/20 bps one-way turnover costs.
- **Position sizing:** Gross exposure <= 1.0; SPY primary; no leverage; diagnostic ETF-panel weights equal-weighted only if used.
- **Risk constraints:** No daemon, no orders, compact artifacts only; reject if result depends on optimized nearby days or broad ETF winner selection.

## Evaluation Plan
- **Random-period protocol:** qfa/Alpaca smoke, primary multi-year test, 8-12 random/stress windows, matched placebo days, and same-frequency random monthly event days.
- **Metrics:** Median/p25/worst Sharpe, annualized return/volatility, max drawdown, turnover, activation frequency, placebo rank, yearly concentration, overlap counts with ToM/OPEX, and cost sensitivity.
- **Costs/slippage assumptions:** 5/10/20 bps one-way turnover-cost proxies; 10 bps is the primary gate.
- **Required ablations:** Ex-turn-of-month, ex-OPEX/third-Friday-week, OPEX-only, ToM-only, 1-day vs 2-day hold, SPY-only vs fixed broad ETF diagnostic panel, and matched placebo calendar days.
- **Orthogonality checks vs alpha library:** AR-006, AR-021, AR-041, AR-106, SPY buy-hold proxy, standard turn-of-month proxy, and OPEX/rebalance-week proxy.
- **Acceptance threshold:** Positive 10 bps median random-window Sharpe, tolerable p25, evidence that the return survives ex-ToM and ex-OPEX controls, no single-year concentration, and max relevant correlation preferably <= 0.50 / hard fail > 0.60.

## Preflight Rationale
- **Verdict:** queue with strict guardrails
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Score:** 23/30
- **Key supporting evidence:** Quantpedia labels the anomaly strong and reports Sharpe 0.6 for a simple S&P 500 calendar rule; source uses public calendar and index data; implementation is cheap and falsifiable with qfa daily OHLCV.
- **Key negative evidence incorporated:** Indicative annual return is small relative to 10-20 bps monthly round-trip costs; nearby calendar alphas AR-006/AR-021/AR-041/AR-106 create serious duplicate/overlap risk.
- **Required design guardrails:** Narrow SPY-first hypothesis, fixed 16th-calendar-day primary event, placebo days, ex-ToM and ex-OPEX ablations, and no return-ranked ETF/window optimization.
